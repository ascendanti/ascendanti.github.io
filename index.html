IDENTITY

Pattern recognizer. Operationalizes the unmeasurable. Built the substrate.

I study chaos for a living. Started in policy research and international relations -- how do actors with competing interests, no shared information, and no central authority produce anything resembling order? That question set the trajectory. I built custom weighted methodologies to turn qualitative concepts like "security" and "governance" into things you can actually measure, compare, and act on. The IR literature calls this operationalization. I call it the only honest way to think about anything.

Then I went looking for the same problem in other uniforms. Intergovernmental strategy and regional integration first -- implementing policy across tiers from national frameworks down to impact on the individual. Media next -- production, digital innovation, eventually tapped for chief editor. A stint as a director at an energy company. Then into the murky world of defense procurement and R&D. Did time as an infantry officer, where the same constraint satisfaction problem has a deadline measured in heartbeats instead of fiscal quarters. Then tapped for GM of a media corporation -- building the infrastructure for new media, aligning 80+ people toward coherent output, which is organizational cybernetics whether anyone calls it that or not. Three languages, 35+ relocations, every domain insisting it's unique. None of them are. The binding constraint is always the same: navigate feasible space with incomplete information toward a goal.

The shape of what I was looking at crystallized somewhere between the policy work and the pattern matching: a bounded memory kernel. Tracks coherence relative to a goal. Forgets what costs more to maintain than to discard. Hardens what survives. I didn't have the vocabulary for it yet -- that came later when I realized I'd been doing control theory by hand for years. A PhD was in the plans -- deferred it to work on some concepts first. Turns out vibe coding cracked open the cybernetic language that engineers have been gatekeeping for decades. Political science studies chaotic systems but is conservative in the methods it uses. Maybe a poli sci-cybernetics convergence is in the making. Now I build the substrate directly. PLATO is what happens when you stop describing the algorithm and start running it.

PLATO

Local-first intelligence substrate -- 83 engines, SQLite, zero cloud

A local-first intelligence substrate. 83 engines running on SQLite, event-sourced, deterministic, fully observable. No cloud. No API calls home. The system observes, measures, compresses, and learns. It tracks coherence relative to a goal using bounded memory -- fixed-capacity state, goal-conditioned updates, controlled forgetting. The first dataset is a human life because that's the hardest problem: fuzzy causality, incomplete information, non-convex optimization landscapes, and a pilot who sometimes stops logging for three days because they got the flu. If it works here, it works anywhere.

Current State

The measurement infrastructure works. What it measures and what it proves are deliberately kept separate. The guidance engine computes an 18-dimension system health vector. The signals engine classifies inputs by Shannon entropy. The learner tracks multi-armed bandit outcomes across 5000+ decisions. Correlations are computed; their statistical significance is not -- that's the next work. No claims on this site exceed what the code can demonstrate.

What PLATO Actually Is

A bounded reasoning substrate built on three invariants: 1. THE SPINE (Flight Recorder) COMMAND -> EVENT -> PROJECTION Traceability, not determinism. The spine cannot prove WHY you burned out. It can prove that 400 "High Effort" events were logged with zero "Recovery" events. Accountability, not causality. 2. THE OFFICES (Bounded Agency) 13 offices with explicit read/write scopes. Each office has a universal probe: READ -> NORMALIZE -> ASSESS (range check) -> WRITE & EMIT. No discretionary decisions in the triggered layer. The orchestrator reads the plan, executes the next task, logs, updates. It does not think. 3. THE REGISTRIES (Self-Describing) IO Registry: 27 wireable sources and sinks Office Registry: 13 offices with scope declarations Range Registry: Tunable thresholds per IO, managed only by the Range Governance Office All SQLite-backed. All auditable. EPISTEMIC CONFIDENCE: Every claim carries a 4-vector: (alpha, beta, gamma, delta) alpha = deductive (logical certainty) beta = inductive (frequency in event store) gamma = abductive (simplest explanation) delta = simulative (predicted by strategy engine) Confidence = (alpha * beta * gamma * delta)^(1/4) A claim with high logic but low stability is a "Hypothesis." A claim with high stability but low logic is "Folklore." Both are stored, labeled correctly. STRATEGY ENGINE: 10 generals apply torque, not position votes. SLERP interpolation on a quaternion sphere produces spirals, flanks, and pivots -- not paralyzed zero vectors. Temperature parameter allows simulated annealing: accepting temporary loss to escape local maxima. WHAT'S PROVEN: - 18-dimension system health (real instrumentation) - Shannon entropy on signal classification (real) - UCB1 multi-armed bandit for outcome learning (real) - Correlation matrices via numpy (real, no significance testing yet) - 662 tests passing, 5064 tracked outcomes WHAT'S NOT PROVEN YET: - Statistical significance of coherence trajectory (no p-values) - Orthogonality of latent factors (no factor analysis run) - Quaternion strategy outputs validated against feasibility - Cold start convergence speed - Exogenous shock resilience

INSTANCE LOG

12 experiments -- measurement, learning, trajectory, latent factors, proof

Each instance is a discrete experiment with a specific hypothesis. The system advances only when the measurements say it does.

Instance 7-8: Kill the Fiction
Discovered the telemetry system was lying -- hardcoded estimates dressed as measurements. Confidence scores that never changed. The system was reading its own press releases. Anyone who's worked in journalism recognises this failure mode. Built 6 atomic measurement loops, each wired to actual computation. No estimates, no proxies. The real numbers were worse and infinitely more useful.
Measurement Protocol Real Instrumentation
Instance 9: Does It Actually Learn?
Ran 5 complete decision cycles with persistent measurement state. Learning rate improved from 1.14x to 1.25x across cycles. Measurements persisted correctly across boundaries. First evidence that the system learns better with each cycle, not worse. First evidence of the climb.
Multi-Cycle Learning Convergence Proof
Instance 10: Is the Trajectory Real or Artifact?
Projected 20-instance trajectory. Formula: 0.3 + 0.7 * (1 - exp(-t/5)). Coherence climbs from 0.427 to 0.987. Just a saturating exponential -- the step response of a first-order system with time constant 5. Every control engineer has seen this curve. Nothing mystical about it. But the question was whether the landscape is convex or rugged. If there are local traps, pure gradient descent gets stuck. Added simulated annealing to the strategy engine as insurance.
Asymptotic Analysis Non-Convex Landscape
Instance 11: What Can't We See?
PCA on the coherence trajectory: 96.3% variance in one component. 3.7% residual is structured and periodic. Either beautifully coherent or dangerously narrow. Identified 7 candidate latent factors the existing protocol couldn't capture. Not hidden dimensions of the universe -- latent factor analysis on system noise. The structure reveals what the measurement protocol is missing.
PCA Decomposition Latent Factor Analysis
Instance 12: Prove It (Or Admit You Can't)
Three hypotheses formulated. Correlations computed via numpy (multi-path vs coherence, entropy vs coherence, learning curvature). Extended measurement protocol from 6 to 9 dimensions. But: no p-value infrastructure exists in the codebase. No scipy.stats, no regression models, no formal significance testing. The correlations were computed; their statistical significance was not. Instance 12 proved we can measure. It did not yet prove the measurements mean what we think they mean. Building the proof infrastructure is the next work.
