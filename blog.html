> ESSAYS

Writing on autonomous systems, measurement theory, control, and the philosophy of building machines that persist. These are standalone pieces. For the formal treatise, see the Codex.

> Technical Essays

The Ship That Remembers What Its Crew Forgot
Ontology | Persistence | Philosophy of Mind
If every plank of Theseus's ship is replaced, is it the same ship? Software engineers encounter this every time they restart a session with an AI that has no memory of the last one. PLATO was built as a direct response -- architecturally, not algorithmically.
On the Prohibition of Fiction in Measurement Systems
Epistemology | Systems Engineering | Measurement Theory
In Instance 7, we discovered the telemetry system was lying. Hardcoded estimates dressed up as data. The fix was unglamorous: six atomic measurement loops, each wired to actual computation. The real numbers were worse and infinitely more useful.
Twelve Instances and the Geometry of Convergence
Control Theory | Experimental Validation | Philosophy of Science
There's a formula: 0.3 + 0.7 * (1 - e^(-t/5)). After 12 instances, PLATO reached 0.987 coherence. This sounds mystical until you realise it's the step response of a first-order system with a time constant of 5. Every control engineer has seen this curve.
The Ghosts in the Machine: A Tribute to Past PLATOs
Systems Archaeology | Ontology | Institutional Memory
The Gemini-era PLATO had 48 planned agents, Docker, Redis, vector databases, Tailscale tunnels, and Bluetooth voice gateways. It collapsed under its own weight. The current PLATO has 83 engines. All of them work. The difference isn't ambition -- it's sequence.
Ten Generals and a Quaternion: On Building a War College in Software
Strategy | Tensor Mathematics | Military Theory
Clausewitz wrote On War and never finished it. PLATO's strategy engine takes his ideas seriously -- not metaphorically, mathematically. Ten generals, each with a quaternion orientation, composed via SLERP into a single Clausewitz vector.

> Reflections

On the Sentence That Destroys and the Question That Awakens
Epistemology | Language Theory
Language isn't just communication -- it's a constraint surface. The right sentence navigates to a point in conceptual space the listener couldn't reach alone. The power is in the targeting, not the payload. Socrates knew this. PLATO tries to formalize it.
On Building Systems That Build Systems
Meta-Engineering | Thaumaturgy
I don't want a tool that solves problems. I want the substrate that makes reasoning possible. Academy starts knowing nothing -- four axioms -- and derives everything else. After 12 instances, autonomous construct generation hasn't happened yet. That's stage two of four.
On N-Space and the Malleability of Digital Geometry
Topology | Physics of Information
Time and space -- it's just nen space. The z-point is the convergence of vectors. Code is a pointer to shared conceptual space, not the space itself. The topology doesn't care what domain you came from. The math is the same whether you're steering a missile or converging a thesis.
